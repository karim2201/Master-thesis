\chapter{Literature Review Methodology}

\section{Purpose of the Review}
The purpose of this literature review is to analyze how Digital Twin technology is defined, structured, and applied in eHealth. The review focuses on general healthcare applications while also examining the cardiovascular field as a representative clinical domain where Digital Twin research is relatively advanced. It aims to identify conceptual foundations, architectures, enabling technologies, application categories, cardiovascular use cases, and open challenges.

\section{Research Questions}
The review is guided by the following research questions:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{RQ1:} What are the main definitions and components of Digital Twin technology?
    \item \textbf{RQ2:} How is Digital Twin technology applied in healthcare and eHealth?
    \item \textbf{RQ3:} What architectures have been proposed for healthcare Digital Twins?
    \item \textbf{RQ4:} What technologies enable Digital Twin-based eHealth systems?
    \item \textbf{RQ5:} What are the main challenges and limitations of Digital Twins in eHealth?
    \item \textbf{RQ6:} How is Digital Twin technology used in the cardiovascular field, and what can this domain teach us about patient-specific and clinically interpretable eHealth systems?
\end{itemize}

\section{Search Strategy}
The literature search is based on combinations of the following keywords:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item ``Digital Twin'';
    \item ``Digital Twin in Healthcare'';
    \item ``Digital Twin for Health'';
    \item ``Digital Twin eHealth'';
    \item ``Patient-Specific Digital Twin'';
    \item ``Healthcare Digital Twin Architecture'';
    \item ``Digital Twin Ethics Healthcare'';
    \item ``Digital Twin Interoperability Healthcare'';
    \item ``Digital Twin Personalized Medicine'';
    \item ``Digital Twin Remote Monitoring'';
    \item ``Digital Twin Hospital Workflow'';
    \item ``Cardiovascular Digital Twin'';
    \item ``Heart Failure Digital Twin'';
    \item ``Hemodynamic Digital Twin''.
\end{itemize}

The selected papers include review articles, architecture proposals, conceptual studies, and application-oriented research related to Digital Twins in healthcare. Cardiovascular papers were added because they provide concrete examples of multimodal, patient-specific, and physiology-informed Digital Twin development.

\section{Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria}
\subsection{Inclusion Criteria}
Papers are included if they satisfy at least one of the following criteria:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item they define or discuss Digital Twin concepts and maturity levels;
    \item they focus on Digital Twin applications in healthcare or eHealth;
    \item they propose a healthcare Digital Twin architecture;
    \item they discuss enabling technologies such as IoT, AI, simulation, cloud, edge computing, or interoperability standards;
    \item they analyze ethical, legal, privacy, or security challenges in healthcare Digital Twins;
    \item they present a patient-specific, organ-specific, disease-specific, process-oriented, or public-health Digital Twin;
    \item they provide a cardiovascular Digital Twin example involving biosignals, imaging, hemodynamics, heart failure, or interpretable AI.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Exclusion Criteria}
Papers are excluded if they are not related to Digital Twins, if they focus only on non-healthcare domains without transferable concepts, if they use the term Digital Twin only as a synonym for a static model without any data connection, or if they provide insufficient methodological, architectural, or clinical detail.

\section{Classification Criteria}
The selected papers are classified according to:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item application domain;
    \item type of Digital Twin;
    \item clinical or operational use case;
    \item data sources;
    \item architecture layers;
    \item enabling technologies;
    \item modeling approach, such as mechanistic simulation, AI, hybrid modeling, or rule-based reasoning;
    \item services provided, such as monitoring, prediction, simulation, treatment planning, or workflow optimization;
    \item maturity level;
    \item security and privacy considerations;
    \item clinical validation strategy;
    \item limitations and future directions.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
This chapter defined the methodology used to analyze Digital Twin research for eHealth. The review was expanded to include general healthcare applications and cardiovascular Digital Twin studies. The next chapter applies these criteria to compare selected studies and extract the main trends.
